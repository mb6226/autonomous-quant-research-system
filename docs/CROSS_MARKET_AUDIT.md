Questions & Analysis
--------------------

1) Why is Extra Trees champion?

- Extra Trees has the highest overall_average_accuracy (0.54345) because it substantially outperformed LightGBM on BTCUSDT, USOIL and US30 by ~3.9–4.1 percentage points, which offsets its weaker results on EURUSD and slightly weaker on XAUUSD.

2) Did Extra Trees win all markets or was one market dominant?

- Extra Trees did NOT win all markets. Per-market wins vs LightGBM:
  - BTCUSDT: ET +3.89% (0.54444 vs 0.50556)
  - EURUSD: ET -4.76% (0.47631 vs 0.52394) — loss
  - XAUUSD: ET -0.65% (0.50376 vs 0.51022) — slight loss
  - USOIL: ET +4.25% (0.63065 vs 0.58817)
  - US30: ET +4.14% (0.56210 vs 0.52070)

- Thus ET wins 3/5 markets; its margin is driven by strong performance on BTCUSDT, USOIL and US30 rather than dominance on a single market.

3) How large is the margin versus LightGBM?

- Overall margin: ET - LightGBM = 0.54345 - 0.52972 = +0.01373 (~1.37 percentage points).
- Per-market margins listed above; three markets show ~3.9–4.3pp advantages which drive the overall margin.

4) How large is the margin versus MLP?

- Overall margin: ET - MLP = 0.54345 - 0.51564 = +0.02781 (~2.78 percentage points).

5) Is the result statistically meaningful?

- Short answer: NOT yet. Reasons:
  - Artifacts were generated by the test harness with sampling enabled (the cross-market run used `allow_sampling=True` and `sample_frac=0.2` in the test), so these are sampled, order-preserving subsamples — not full-dataset WFV runs. Sampling inflates variability and can change ranking.
  - The per-market accuracies vary considerably (per-model std across markets is large — e.g., ExtraTrees population std across markets ≈ 0.05–0.06). The overall margin vs LightGBM (~0.0137) is smaller than the inter-market variability, so the difference is borderline relative to noise introduced by sampling and market heterogeneity.
  - No seed-averaging (stability) was performed for the cross-market run; single-run averages across folds and markets were used.

Consistency Checks
------------------

- Was `AQRS_SAMPLE_FRAC` used? The artifact does not embed an explicit flag, but the cross-market validator in tests invoked `CrossMarketValidator(allow_sampling=True, sample_frac=0.2)`. Therefore these artifacts were generated with sampling (subsampling factor ~0.2). If you re-run with `allow_sampling=False` and full data, results may change.

- Was `allow_sampling=False` used? No — current artifacts were created with `allow_sampling=True` (test harness). Production-grade cross-market validation should use `allow_sampling=False`.

- Were all models evaluated on the same markets? Yes — each model's `per_market_results` contains entries for BTCUSDT, EURUSD, XAUUSD, USOIL, US30 (fold count shows 4 folds each).

- Were all models evaluated with `WalkForwardValidator`? Yes — the validator run produced 4 folds per market (train_stops default), and `folds`==4 for each per-market result.

Conclusion
----------

B) Cross-Market result invalid — do NOT treat Extra Trees as a confirmed, production-level champion based on these artifacts alone.

Rationale:
- Artifacts were generated using sampling (test harness with `allow_sampling=True`, `sample_frac=0.2`) which can materially alter rankings.
- Inter-market variability is large; the overall margin vs LightGBM (~1.37pp) is smaller than observed variability across markets.
- No stability/seed averaging was performed for these cross-market runs.

Recommended Remediation (next steps)
------------------------------------

1. Re-run cross-market validation with `allow_sampling=False` (full datasets) and persist artifacts.
2. For each model, run stability validator (seed averaging) to measure std_accuracy across seeds; include these in ranking decisions or compute confidence intervals.
3. Consider aggregating results using hierarchical metrics (e.g., weighted by market liquidity or by significance) or performing paired statistical tests across folds/markets (paired t-test or Wilcoxon signed-rank) to confirm significance of overall margin.
4. If Extra Trees remains top after full-data, stability-aware runs, and statistical tests, promote to Champion status within governance (subject to governance rules).
